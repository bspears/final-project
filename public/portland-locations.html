The Crystal Ballroom

Venue

The Crystal Ballroom offers music goers an experience all its own, boasting the nations only mechanically “floating” dancefloor. Offering a wide variety of music, it also houses its own hotel and bar within the venue.

The Crystal Ballroom, which is owned by McMenamins, is one of Portland’s oldest music venues, serving as a concert hall for over 90 years. The Crystal first opened its doors in 1914 as a premier Ballroom, and was a dance-revival hall during the Great Depression. During the 1960’s it was host to some of the biggest names in rock and roll, including an often-debated rumor that Little Richard fired Jimmi Hendrix during a gig here. Since McMenamins’ purchase in 1997, the dance floor has been returned to its original working order, and is the only “floating” dance floor left in the US. The dance floor is mechanically elevated from its base, allowing dancers the feeling of “dancing on clouds”.

Address: 1332 W. Burnside., Portland, OR 97209

Irving Street Kitchen

Restaurant

Located in the heart of the Pearl district in portland, Irving Street features cuisine highlighting the local abundance of the Pacific NW. Offering a more casual fine dining experience, its great for a nice dinner out with friends or a romantic evening for two.

Irving Street offers a casual fine dining experience, featuring cuisine highlighting the natural abundance of the Pacific Northwest under the guidance of executive chef Sarah Chafer. Chafer started her culinary career in New York, moving once to San Francisco before accepting the executive chef position at Irving Street. Irving street features a large open seating area with a centrally-located bar with an artistic animal fresco overhang.

Address: 701 NW 13th Ave, Portland, OR 97209

Saturday Market

Venue

Every saturday and sunday during the summer and fall Portland offers a saturday market, nestled on the waterfront of downtown. Local vendors set up stalls and sell wares and food, as well as often offering live entertainment during the afternoon.

Portland’s Saturday Market boasts an impressive amount of stalls and vendors selling a wide array of products, all of which are locally hand-made. Within the market there are many stalls selling a variety of foods, from middle eastern Kabobs to smoked turkey legs and mac-n-cheese. Most weekends feature some form of live entertainment, from local musical acts to comedians. The Market is a seasonal event running most of the year, opening in early March and running until late December.

Address: 2 SW Natio Pkwy, Portland OR 97204
