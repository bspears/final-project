Space Needle

Landmark, Restaurant

Seattle's most recognizable landmark, the Space Needle, offers visitors an impressive rotating view of the city as well as a great meal in its signature restaurant, SkyCity.

Climb 520 feet to the observation deck of Seattle's signature building, which is 605 feet tall, for spectacular views of the city and its surrounding area. For food and fantastic sights check out SkyCity, the tower's rotating restaurant that makes one complete orbit per hour.

Address: 400 Broad Street Seattle, WA 98109

Gas Works Park

Outdoor

Gas Works Park embodies Seattle's dedication to its citizens. Formerly an industrial eyesore, it has now been converted into a beautiful public park for all to enjoy.

From industrial sludge to family fun: this urban park features an industrial plant-turned kiddie play area as well as a beautiful lake and picnic area. Gas Works Park is a fantastic place to watch the annual fireworks displays on the Space Needle on the 4th of July and New Year’s Eve.

Address: 3300 Meridian Ave. N, Seattle, WA 98103

Pike Place Market

Landmark, Outdoor

Pike Place Market is Seattle's premiere open market, offering the freshest of the Pacific's bounty as well as locally-sourced produce.

The Market opened August 17, 1907, and is one of the oldest continuously operated public farmers' markets in the United States. One of the few authentic farmer's markets in the United States, this hub of delicious scents, delectable eats and delightful personalities attracts nine million visitors a year.

Address: First Ave., Seattle, WA 98101
