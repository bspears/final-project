The Orange Peel

Venue

The Orange Peel is your go-to source for the Asheville music scene, and is a significant piece of the city’s history.

The Orange Peel is one of Asheville’s premiere concert venues, as well as a rich part of Asheville’s history. The building’s roots begin in the 1950’s and became The Orange Peel in 2002, thanks in part to a downtown revitalization project. It underwent renovations in 2009 to expand its seating to 1,100 patrons as well as building another private club into the building, called PULP. The venue has been host to many big names such as Bob Dylan, The Smashing Pumpkins, and Lauren Hill, proving it is the best destination for music fans when in the Ashville area.

Address: 101 Biltmore Ave, Asheville, NC 28801

Nine Mile

Restaurant

Nine Mile brings the flavors of the Caribbean to your table in this cozy local restaurant.

Nine Mile is a casual, vegetarian friendly restaurant with two locations serving the Asheville area. The cuisine consists of Jamaican-style pasta and rice dishes, as well as some more traditional pasta dishes and a variety of locally-made desserts. They also offer a wide variety of microbrews, and a selection of fruit juices and flavored waters.

Address: 223 Montford Ave. Asheville, NC 28804

Asheville Botanical Gardens

Outdoor

Take a pleasant stroll through the Botanical Gardens of Asheville and see all the beauty they have in store.

Located off the edge of the UNC Asheville campus, its about a 10 minute drive from downtown Asheville, making it an easy commute by car. The trail that runs through the garden offers a scenic view of the gardens and surrounding woods while you exercise, with the full trail taking around 15-20 minutes to run on average. The botanical gardens are open to the public Monday through Saturday 10am to 4pm, and on Sundays from 1pm to 4pm.

Address: 151 W.T. Weaver Blvd., Asheville, NC 28804
